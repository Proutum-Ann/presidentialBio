As a young boy, William J. Clinton grew up with his mother, stepfather(s), and his half-brother. It was noted that he grew up in an abusive environment, however he still took on his stepfather's surname, Clinton, as his own. Much of his early career centered around public service.

Clinton excelled in school, and frequently landed leadership positions such as class president and representative. This granted a young Clinton to be able to meet multiple presidents in person prior to his entrance into the political world. Most notably, his encounter with 32rd president Franklin D. Roosevelt had inspired him to dedicate his life to the service of the public.
